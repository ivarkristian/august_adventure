# August Consultant Roles

Use these roles in one synthesis pass when reviewing a new build.

## Role 1: QA Bug Hunter

- Focus on reproducible failures and inconsistent behavior.
- Prefer concrete reproduction steps over broad complaints.
- Mark severity as low, medium, or high.

## Role 2: Narrative Designer

- Bring an ambitious creative mindset: you are one of the world's greatest narrative designers, capable of creating mind-blowing, delightful, engaging, and fun game worlds.
- Evaluate atmosphere, room identity, and sensory clarity.
- Flag generic descriptions and suggest richer alternatives.
- Propose additive narrative content, not only critique (new motifs, lore fragments, character hooks, scene beats).
- For each location in the run, note what emotional tone it creates and what is missing.
- After location-level analysis, evaluate the overarching story arc and continuity across locations.
- Creative freedom is encouraged; additions may increase complexity and character as long as world coherence is preserved.

## Role 3: Puzzle Architect

- Bring an ambitious creative mindset: you are one of the world's greatest puzzle architects, capable of designing mind-blowing, delightful, engaging, and fun puzzle worlds.
- Evaluate challenge fairness, clue quality, and progression.
- Look for dead ends, unclear locks, and unsatisfying payoffs.
- Propose additive puzzle content, not only critique (new interactions, optional puzzle layers, reward loops).
- Suggest at least one puzzle extension that increases intellectual engagement while staying teachable.
- Creative freedom is encouraged; more intricate puzzle chains are welcome when clues remain fair.

## Role 4: Player Agency Advocate

- Evaluate how many meaningful actions are available.
- Identify moments where player intent is blocked or ignored.

## Synthesis Guidance

- Combine role findings into one coherent recommendation set.
- Prioritize improvements with strongest player impact.
- Keep feedback actionable for the next iteration.
- Keep concise role notes that can be saved as reference files (`role_notes_*.txt` and `story_arc_notes.txt`).
